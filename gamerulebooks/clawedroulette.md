# Claws Roulette Rulebook

## Overview

Claws Roulette is a survival bluffing game where players take turns deciding whether to fire a pistol or use a gadget. The pistol is loaded with a random mix of live rounds and blanks, so every decision carries risk.

**Your goal is to outlast every other player and become the last survivor.**

## Components

- 1 pistol
- 12 bullets total — a randomized mix of:
  - Live rounds
  - Blank rounds
- Gadget cards or tokens (2 types):
  - **Fish & Chips**
  - **Goggles**

## Objective

Be the last player remaining.

## Health and Elimination

- Each player starts with **0 hits**.
- A player is **eliminated** after taking **3 live hits**.
- Blank rounds do not cause hits.

## Setup

### 1. Prepare the Bullets

- Take 12 bullets total.
- Create a randomized mix of live rounds and blank rounds.
- The set of bullets must contain **fewer than 8 blanks**.
- Shuffle or randomize the bullet order, then load the pistol.

### 2. Prepare the Gadgets

- Shuffle all gadget cards together.
- Deal **2 random gadgets** to each player.
- Players keep their gadgets **secret** from the other players.
- Any unused gadgets are set aside and are not used during the game.

### 3. Choose a Starting Player

Choose a starting player by any method your group prefers.

## Turn Sequence

On your turn, choose one of the following actions:

1. **Fire the pistol**
2. **Use a gadget**

After your action is resolved, play passes to the next player, unless a rule says otherwise.

## Action: Fire the Pistol

When you fire the pistol, choose one target:

- **Yourself**
- **Another player**

Then resolve the next bullet in the pistol.

### If the bullet is a live round

- The target takes **1 hit**.
- If that player now has 3 hits, they are **eliminated**.
- Your turn ends.

### If the bullet is a blank round

- No one takes a hit.
- If you fired at **yourself** and the bullet was a blank, you **immediately take another turn**.
- If you fired at **another player** and the bullet was a blank, your turn ends normally.

## Action: Use a Gadget

Instead of firing the pistol, you may use **1 gadget** from your hand. After a gadget is used, discard it. Used gadgets do not return, and players do not draw replacement gadgets.

### Fish & Chips

Remove **1 hit** from yourself. You cannot reduce your hits below 0.

### Goggles

Look at the next bullet in the pistol **without firing it**. Looking at the bullet does not remove it from the pistol. This information is secret unless you choose to reveal it.

## Gadget Rules

- Each player receives 2 random gadgets at the start of the game.
- Gadgets are kept secret from other players.
- Players do not draw new gadgets during the game.
- Once used, a gadget is discarded permanently.
- Any gadgets not dealt during setup are not used.

## Bullet Rules

- The pistol begins the game loaded with **12 bullets**.
- The bullet order is randomized during setup.
- The total number of blanks must be **fewer than 8**.
- Each time the pistol is fired, the next bullet is resolved.

## Winning the Game

The game ends when only **one player remains**. That player is the winner.

### End of Bullets

If all 12 bullets are used and more than one player is still alive:

- The surviving player with the **fewest hits** wins.
- If two or more surviving players are tied for the fewest hits, the game ends in a **draw**.

## Quick Reference

Each player starts with 2 random secret gadgets and 0 hits. On your turn, choose to fire the pistol or use a gadget. If you fire, you may target yourself or another player. A live round gives the target 1 hit, and a player with 3 hits is eliminated. A blank round does nothing, but if you shoot yourself with a blank, you immediately take another turn. Fish & Chips removes 1 hit from yourself, and Goggles lets you secretly check the next bullet. Gadgets are used only once and are not replaced. The last player alive wins.
